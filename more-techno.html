Different Types of Techno:

Like other music genres, the rise of techno music was also followed by its sub-genres birth. Many artists created their own characteristics as they want to show people what makes them different. This made them break a lot of rules, and that's how sub-genres were born.

Some of the types of techno are:

Minimal Techno:

It is characterized by a stripped-down aesthetic that exploits the use of repetition and understated development. Minimal techno is thought to have been originally developed in the early 1990s by Detroit-based producers Robert Hood and Daniel Bell. My favourite representative of this sub-genre is Boris Brejcha.

Acid Techno:

Acid techno, sometimes known generally as "acid", is a genre of techno that was derived from acid house and developed in Europe in the late 1980s to early 1990s. It saw younger artists apply the "squelching" synthesizer sound of Chicago acid house to harder-edged techno material. Early exponents of the style included Richie Hawtin (aka Plastikman), Aphex Twin, Dave Clarke, Hardfloor, solarquest, and Damon Wilde.

Industrial Techno:

Industrial techno is a subgenre of techno and industrial dance music that originated in the 1990s. Characteristically, it incorporates influences from the bleak, noisy sound and aesthetics of early industrial music acts, particularly Cabaret Voltaire and Throbbing Gristle. The genre has seen a resurgence in the 2010s and it has gained a significant fanbase from the post-dubstep audience. Some artists associated with industrial techno are Helena Hauff, Surgeon, Blawan, Jeff Mills, etc.

Ambient Techno:

Ambient techno (sometimes also called intelligent techno) is an offshoot of techno that united the atmospheric textures of ambient music with the rhythmic elements and production of techno and house. It was pioneered by 1990s electronic artists such as Aphex Twin, Carl Craig, Pete Namlook and Biosphere.

Hardcore Techno:

Hardcore is rooted in the 1970s and early 1980s industrial music, specifically the elements of hard electronic dance music.It is distinguished by faster tempos and a distorted sawtooth kick (160 to 200 BPM or more), the intensity of the kicks and the synthesized bass (in some subgenres), the rhythm and the atmosphere of the themes (sometimes violent), the usage of saturation and experimentation close to that of industrial dance music.
